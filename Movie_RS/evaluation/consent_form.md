## 1. Purpose of the study

You are invited to participate in a class-project evaluation comparing
two versions of a movie recommender website. The study aims to
measure how well each version helps users discover relevant movies
and how satisfying the experience is.

## 2. What you will do

1. Read and sign this consent form.
2. Interact with **two** versions of the site (order is randomised),
   spending roughly 10 minutes per version. During the interaction
   you will:
   - pick favourite genres,
   - rate at least 10 movies,
   - browse the recommendations and provide like / not-interested
     feedback.
3. Fill in a short (8-question Likert + 2 open questions) survey
   after each version.

Total time: approximately **25–30 minutes**.

## 3. Data we record

- The Likert-scale answers to the eight survey questions.
- Your free-text answers to the two open questions.
- The order in which you saw the two versions.

We do **not** record your name, e-mail, IP address, or any
personally-identifying information. Each participant is labelled by
an anonymous ID (e.g. `P03`). No cookies from the system are kept
after the session — the team clicks "Reset" between runs.

## 4. Voluntary participation

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may stop at any time
without giving a reason and without consequence. If you decide to
withdraw, your partial answers will be deleted immediately.

## 5. Risks and benefits

There are no foreseen risks beyond those of ordinary web browsing.
The data is used only for this coursework project; no commercial use
is made. You will not be compensated, but you help improve a
class-project prototype.

## 6. Data retention

Answers are stored in an anonymised CSV file on the team's local
machine and the project's GitHub repository (private). The data
will be destroyed at the end of the semester.
